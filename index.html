Portfolio

About

Socioeconomic factors towards school shootings in America is an important issue because it impacts so many students' lives. From 2010-2019, there have been 318 school shootings. Families mourning the loss of their children, students being afraid to step back into a classroom. The simple act of attending school has become a fear inducing endeavor. As students ourselves, the idea of an event like this happening in our own institution is incredibly frightening. Acknowledging how these factors influence school gun violence can lead to a better understanding of why they are happening and how they can be prevented.

GAO analysis of the Naval Postgraduate School's K-12 School Shooting Database.

This chart comes from GAO analysis of the Naval Postgraduate School’s K-12 School Shooting Database for school years 2009-2010 through 2018-2019. The graph above is a chart that gives a visual of factors that have influenced school shootings. The chart shows how the motives of school shootings can vary greatly. There is a common trend in school shootings however, shown in the chart that most start from grieving students or people. 99 or 31% stem from conflicts or fights, including gang related violence on school grounds. Knowing this is a major factor in school shootings can lead to a better understanding of how to prevent them in the future.

Students Mourn the Loss of Their Friends.

This photo shows three Denver area high school students comforting one another at a candlelight vigil at Civic Center Park in Denver, Colorado on April 21st, 1999. The picture shows the aftermath of a school shooting and reveals how devastating the impact is to the people around them. It can be especially be seen through the facial expressions of the three young teenagers, how they are filled with sadness, mourning, and a feeling of denial that this tragic event had happened, and how it cost them the lives of their dear friends. Furthermore, the picture also provides the implications of this event, how it took a toll on the minds and mental health of these teenagers. Especiallly, knowing that biologically, teenage years are the times where adolescents transition to adults and they experience a lot of changes in their life. Having to experience this unfortunate event will leave a traumatic mark on their hearts for the rest of their lives. This picture brings awareness of how much of an impact this event could bring. It creates an emotinal appeal towards people regarding how hurtful it is to experience school shootings and may further create a sense of call to action to start making changes regarding this particular issue to prevent an event like this happening again.

The Impacts of Bullying

This picture illustrates bullying, which is a big reason for school shootings. This common trait can be traced in almost all cases of mass shootings carried out by adolescents. In fact, most reports of school shootings find that the shooter experienced challenges at home, bullying, and/or received some form of disciplinary action for their behavior because of it. The effects of bullying isolates these perpetrators into dark and dangerous environments, which eventually leads to violent retaliation. Lack of resources to combat bullying in schools puts these communities at higher risk. Socioeconomic factors come into play in this issue because under diagnosis of mental health issues barely get addressed within these at risk students, usually those who come from lower income backgrounds.
